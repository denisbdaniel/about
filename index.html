Dionysius: Metaphysicus

Denis B. Daniel

Dionysius: Metaphysicus is an amalgamation of philosophical musings, introspective ideals, and a departure from traditional conventions, all through the lens of the protagonist, Dionysius — an embodiment of the author's unconscious desires, characterized by contradiction, neurosis, and delusion. 

Dionysius, not to be confused with the Greek God "Dionysus”, is a metaphysical individual that had been hiding within his mind for all of his existence, however, one day, a desire for understanding the external world had struck — a new hope that seeks a deeper comprehension of the world that surrounds him.

This fictional exploration of metaphysics is not bound by linear objectives but rather flows as a continuous stream of consciousness, encompassing the author's aspiration for a heightened perception and understanding.
 

 

Sempiternus: Rebirth

Denis B. Daniel

Transcending metaphysical dogmas and incorporating cultural Christianity within the individual's framework of intuition and logical dependencies.

Offering a departure from conventional theology, collectivist metaphysics, herd-minded mentalities, presenting a version of Christianity that empowers the individual.

Emphasizing the idea of overmen, who take charge of their lives independently, in an iconoclastic manner.

 

As we stand on the precipice of an era where the belief in God seems to have waned in the hearts of many, methodical religion gradually loses its grip; yet I am afraid that the death of God is but a wasted sentiment, for these men of modernity cannot help but assert a different God, one that encompasses but secular dogmatism, these men are not mere followers — but whole herds of followers, which we have so often seen before.

It is crucial to recall that the profound essence of Christianity does not solely reside in its dogmatism, but rather in the cultural treasures that it has nurtured throughout history. While the classical tenets and doctrines of Christianity may have played a role in shaping its identity, it is the prosperous tapestry of art, literature, music, architecture, and societal values that have woven an enduring cultural legacy. The masterpieces of Renaissance art, the chants of Gregorian plainsong, and the awe-inspiring cathedrals and churches that have stood for centuries — these stand as articulations of the vibrant, superior Christian culture that transcends sheer theological beliefs.

By eliminating the dogmatic element and delving into the potency of Christianity, we can find vision, meaning, and a connection to something greater than our mere selves — a connection to metaphysical culture, a testament to the enduring power of its intuitive, intellectual, and moral contributions.

Secularism has failed my generation — the man of modernity is far too ignorant; incapable of asserting meaning, purpose and a greater vision for himself. But for men with a desire for greatness, I may hold an antidote. With a desire for reaching a state further than Jupiter itself, an assertion of Christianity, refusal of dogmatism, be it theological or secular, and its synthesis within the individual’s perception is required.
