The Aphantasic Psyche and Its Proclivity to Creativity

Description
In this paper, D. B. Denis, an independent researcher, explores the implications of Aphantasia on consciousness, subconsciousness, dreams, and personality traits through three distinct autoethnographic studies. The research investigates the potential of achieving arousal despite the lack of conscious imagery, delves into the role of dreams in Aphantasic individuals, and examines the temperamental implications of Aphantasia using the Big Five personality model. The findings suggest that individuals with Aphantasia can experience arousal through abstract cognitive processing, have vivid and surreal dreams despite the absence of conscious imagery, and show correlations between certain personality traits and the vividness of unconscious imagery during dreams.
